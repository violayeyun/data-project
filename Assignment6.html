Feature 1: gap ratio between state total discharges to each provider¶

This feature enable us to detect the difference between the provider charges and state average total charges according to a same DRG.The gap to average level could embody the charge level at some degree. Abuse detection might be easy to spotify due to some providers charge more than average level.

Feature 2: gap ratio between state max discharges to each provider¶

This feature enable us to detect the difference between the provider charges and state max charges according to a same DRG.The gap to max level could embody the reasonal probability of the charge level at some degree. Abuse detection might be adjust that some providers might charge more than average level for their private pharmacy attributes.

Feature 3: gap ratio between state min covered charges to each provider¶

This feature enable us to detect the difference between the provider charges and state min charges according to a same DRG.The gap to min level could embody the reasonal probability of the charge level at some degree. Abuse detection might be adjust that some providers might charge more than average level for their private pharmacy attributes.

Feature 4: Type of provider_ One-hot¶

This feature simplily classify the provider as hospital or other type, providers'name which included hospital, for example "xxx hospital" or "xxx hospital Medical Center" will both classify as hospital. Since Hospital is that where pts are admitted, and also more than a half people believe hospital have a wider range of services, seperate provider is necessity. And this feature might also support modeling into a more detail level.

Feature 5: Personal non-medicare payment ratio¶

This feature embody the charge system at another site. Chances are, the abuse of physician might be they made a order which not covered in the medicare. It might due to the low profit of medicine covered by medicare plan. In order to detect this kind of problem, it's better to specify a factor as person non-medicare payment for further exploration.

Feature 6: Personal non-covered payment ratio¶

This feature is established based on feature 13. Chances are, the abuse of physician might be they made a order which not covered in the health plan. It might due to the relationship of health insurance company and provider. In order to detect this kind of problem, it's better to specify a factor as person non-covered payment for further exploration.

Feature 7: The percentage of charge (number) higher than average level for providers¶

This feature embody the high-probability overcharge cases and take total cases into consideration. The ratio feature avoid the effect of large volume to some degree. Besides, with higher percentage means the provider have higher probability to experience abuse.

Feature 8: Discharge to medicare payment ratio¶

This feature generates from a common ratio: Charge-to-medicare-payment-ratio,which could detect how they charges compared to medicare plan, and with limited data, I used total discharge amount, it could also show their relationship to some degree.

X_train_norm, X_test_norm = standardizer(x_train, x_test)
# Test a range of k-neighbors from 15 to 225. There will be 15 k-NN models.
n_clf = 15
k_list = [15, 30, 45, 60, 75, 90, 105, 120, 
 135, 150, 165, 180, 195, 210, 225]
# Just prepare data frames so we can store the model results
train_scores = np.zeros([x_train.shape[0], n_clf])
test_scores = np.zeros([x_test.shape[0], n_clf])
train_scores.shape
# Modeling
for i in range(n_clf):
    k = k_list[i]
    clf = KNN(n_neighbors=k, method='largest')
    clf.fit(X_train_norm)

    # Store the results in each column:
    train_scores[:, i] = clf.decision_scores_
    test_scores[:, i] = clf.decision_function(X_test_norm) 
# Decision scores have to be normalized before combination
train_scores_norm, test_scores_norm = standardizer(train_scores,test_scores)

Business insights¶

Based on these model above, all of them are simmilar for the variables explaination. Although the number has been standardize, we can also figure out someting: These results show that for the type of provider, we can easily see that it's absolute mean value is lower than other group, which means most of institutions are not belongs to hospital. It might because the institution without hospital in name experience less regulation compared with official hospital, thus, it's more likely to have abuse or fraud in their treatment. For the discharge to non-medicare payment ratio, it also shows distinguished gap between outliers and normal data. So this feature might need most of our attention, because it ranks the 2nd highest weight among all the features and it means that there might have high possibility that if hospital overcharge on non-medicare part, they might have fraud in this case. Besides, for the gap ratio, it's not obvious on the average level, but for the max or min level, it shows high performance. It's resonable to compare the extreme value in the range. And it also shows that the average level ratio might cover some fraud actions so we might be careful when next time use average variables in outliers detection. What's more, we can find that the non-covered charges ratio does not perform well. So it might mean it has a poor explaination level in this method. However, the one who plays a biggest part in our model is the variables that calculate the overcharge frequency percentage. This variables takes into consideration that some large hospital have more cases, so it's unfair to calculate overcharge cases. But when I calculated its overcharge than state level frequency percentage, the amount effect was deducted and turns out to be a good predictor.
